36 Why Chaos Engineering Isn't Enough Chaos tools perturb. They break things deliberately. But they don't observe the side effects systematically. You learn that something breaks, not why, not where the cascade propagated. r/devops, LinkedIn
37 Everyone Builds 3 of 6 The six components for living systems — explorer, signal, observer, memory, boundary, coordination. Every tool in the market builds 2-3 and ignores the rest. Nobody connects all six. That's the gap. HN, researchers
38 AI Agents Are Coming to Your Infrastructure. Who Watches Them? AI agents will configure, tune, and manage infrastructure autonomously. Multiple agents, multiple objectives, shared resources. Interference is inevitable at scale. Detection isn't optional — it's survival. LinkedIn, HN
39 The MCP Interface: Talking to Your Infrastructure in Natural Language godon's LLM integration via MCP. Ask questions about your system's coupling, get answers grounded in real detection data. Not a chatbot — an interface to accumulated infrastructure knowledge. r/devops, HN

41 Is godon Already Neuro-Symbolic? LLM reads detection data (neural), applies guardrails and policies (symbolic rules), reports to human who sets constraints (symbolic). The architecture might already be a neuro-symbolic system without calling it that. Researchers
42 Extracting Symbolic Knowledge From Topology Data Detection produces numerical coupling data. What if you extracted symbolic rules — "when parameter X exceeds threshold Y, service Z degrades by 15%"? From data to knowledge to reusable policies. Researchers, contributors
43 Campaign-Driven Optimization: Not Just Tuning, Exploring With Intent A godon campaign isn't a single optimization run. It's a sustained exploration of config space with objectives, constraints, and a lifecycle. The campaign is the unit of work — not a parameter tweak. HN, contributors
44 Policy as the Interface Between Human and System The human doesn't set values. They set policies — constraints, objectives, acceptable ranges. The system interprets and executes. Policy is the control plane for living config. LinkedIn, r/devops
45 Why LLMs Won't Solve This LLMs can reason about config, suggest parameters, even orchestrate changes. But they can't detect interference between autonomous agents acting on shared infrastructure in real time. That requires a signal, an observer, and continuous measurement — not reasoning. HN, LinkedIn
46 The Irreducible Layer No matter how advanced AI gets — better LLMs, more capable agents — the problem of detecting cross-system interference on shared infrastructure doesn't go away. It's structurally irreducible. You can't reason your way to coupling data. You have to measure it. godon's position survives AI advances. Researchers, HN
47 Why LLMs Would Settle on Static Config Again Given the chance, an LLM managing infrastructure would gravitate toward fixed optimal values — a new static config, just AI-generated. The same fallacy, different author. Without exploration and detection, the LLM is just a faster human writing yaml. LinkedIn, HN
48 godon as the Harness for AIOps LLMs are powerful but unreliable. They hallucinate, they drift, they lack grounding. godon provides the structure — guardrails, objectives, boundaries, measurement — that keeps an LLM useful. The LLM suggests, godon validates. The LLM interprets, godon measures. r/devops, LinkedIn
49 Guiding the Dumb LLM: Why AI Ops Needs Structure Today's LLMs aren't smart enough to manage infrastructure autonomously. They need a harness — clear objectives, hard constraints, real-time feedback, measured outcomes. godon is that harness. The LLM doesn't need to understand interference. It just needs to read the detection report. LinkedIn, HN
